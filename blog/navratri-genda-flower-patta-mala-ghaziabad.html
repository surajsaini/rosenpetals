Why We Celebrate Navratri

Navratri is a Hindu festival celebrated for 9 nights to honor Maa Durga and her nine divine forms. The word "Navratri" means "9 nights". It symbolizes the victory of good over evil, spiritual purification, and blessings for health and prosperity. People fast, clean their homes, and decorate beautifully with flowers during this auspicious festival in Ghaziabad.

The Nine Forms of Maa Durga

During Navratri, devotees worship the nine forms of Maa Durga, one form each day:

  • Day 1: Shailaputri – daughter of the mountains
  • Day 2: Brahmacharini – goddess of devotion and penance
  • Day 3: Chandraghanta – goddess of bravery
  • Day 4: Kushmanda – goddess of creativity and energy
  • Day 5: Skandamata – mother of Lord Kartikeya
  • Day 6: Katyayani – goddess of courage
  • Day 7: Kalaratri – goddess of strength
  • Day 8: Mahagauri – goddess of purity
  • Day 9: Siddhidatri – goddess of blessings and wisdom

Decorating your home and mandir with fresh flowers in Ghaziabad during these 9 days brings positivity and blessings of Maa Durga.
